About Pip Hiles

Pip Hiles has worked as a registered nurse in community palliative care for 20 years in Melbourne, Porirua, Wellington, Motueka and Tasman.

Palliative care is end of life care.

Pip has recently trained as a death doula to provide support for people wanting assisted dying at home.

Unlike a registered nurse, a death doula does not attend to medications.

A death doula is present to support the patient, and their whanau in providing a peaceful presence for a gentle, calm, death. With utmost dignity and care.

The end of life phase can be a difficult and fraught time for the person dying.

It is a challenging time for whanau, witnessing the end of life experience of a family member.

The passing of a loved one, can require support to make the transition from dying, to passing away a peaceful experience.

Please contact Pip for any information on death doula services in the home.
